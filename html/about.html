Biography

Travis Scott is a Grammy Award-winning American rapper, singer, songwriter, and record producer from Houston, Texas. Renowned for his innovative sound and electrifying stage presence, he has become one of the most influential artists of his generation.

Scott first gained widespread attention with his critically acclaimed mixtapes Owl Pharaoh and Days Before Rodeo. His debut studio album, Rodeo (2015), cemented his rise to stardom and featured hit singles such as “Antidote” and “Goosebumps.”

Building on that success, he continued to push creative boundaries with his second album, Birds in the Trap Sing McKnight, followed by later projects including the highly anticipated Utopia. Blending elements of hip-hop, R&B, trap, and psychedelic rock, Scott creates a distinctive, immersive sound that sets him apart in contemporary music.
